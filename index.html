Cats Have Always Been With Me

Before I understood memory, I understood fur.
Before I understood loneliness, I understood purring.

Since Childhood

Cats have been part of my entire life. Since childhood, I have always had at least two cats. My earliest memories are not of toys or cartoons — but of warm bodies curled beside me, tiny heartbeats I could feel through my hands.

While my sister stayed home to babysit me, she was often busy doing schoolwork. The house would grow quiet. The afternoons would stretch long. But I was never alone.

My cats would sit beside me, follow me from room to room, or nap close enough that I could hear their breathing. They became my first friends, my first lessons in care, my first understanding of responsibility and love.

Growing Up Together

As I grew, so did they. I learned how to feed them, brush them, comfort them. In return, they taught me gentleness.

They witnessed my childhood tears, my quiet afternoons, my small joys. They never asked questions. They just stayed.

Why Cats Matter

People appreciate cats for many reasons — their quiet companionship, their subtle affection, their unique personalities.

They do not demand attention. They offer presence. And when they choose to sit beside you, that choice feels meaningful.

In Ordinary & Difficult Moments

In ordinary moments — reading, thinking, resting — their softness fills the room with calm.

In difficult moments — when the world feels heavy — their purring becomes steady reassurance. A reminder that love does not need words.

What They Gave Me

  • Comfort without conditions
  • Love expressed quietly but sincerely
  • Lessons in empathy and patience
  • A steady presence through every phase of life

Their independence combined with chosen closeness makes their love feel rare. Their softness, their calm beauty, their purring — all of it turns ordinary life into something gentler.

For the cats who raised me.
For the ones still beside me.
